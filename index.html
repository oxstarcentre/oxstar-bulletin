OxSTaR Bulletin: Teaching, Research & How to Get Involved
 
We're delighted to send out our first bulletin of the new academic year. Here’s a quick round-up of what’s new at OxSTaR and how you can get involved in this term.
 
OxSTaR (Oxford Simulation, Teaching, & Research) is the University of Oxford’s centre for simulation-based education, human factors, and patient-safety research. We design and deliver training for students and multidisciplinary clinical teams, and run research that translates innovation (digital, behavioural, and AI) into safer care at the bedside.
 

We’d really value your views on the bulletin specifically on what we should include or take out so please let us know what you think.

 
Teaching highlights
 
  • Medical student teaching: New Clinical Lecturer Ashley Tomlinson takes over from Nathan Gauge (huge thanks to Nathan for leading the Anaesthetic SSM and much-loved sim sessions).
  • Foundation Year teaching: Long-running programme for our FY1 and 2 trainees, with consistently outstanding feedback and guideline-current scenarios. Quotes include “Most valuable experience in my clinical career so far” and “Best sim session I have done.” Led by Rosie Warren and James Thomas.
  • Virtual Reality: Rapidly grown into an award-winning paediatric programme (leads: Sally Shiels, Nathan Gauge); VR research is a core theme.
  • 360° video intubation: We’re piloting a new immersive 360° filming of intubation to enhance teaching and debrief quality in patients with high consequence infectious diseases (HCID), led by Laura Vincent.
 
Research snapshot
 
We’re scaling fast: 5 DPhil, 2 DM, 1 ACF and 2 Master’s students across clinical education and human factors/patient safety. Active projects span VR, reflective practice, decision-making, digital consent, social networks, AI-assisted decision-making, GP emergency cognitive aids, and incident-investigation quality tools.
 
 
OxCAIRES (Oxford Clinical AI Research for Enhanced Safety): An exciting new cross-disciplinary collaboration designing, evaluating, and translating trustworthy AI to enhance patient safety. OxCAIRES unites engineers at the Institute of Biomedical Engineering (IBME) led by Alison Noble; psychologists in the Department of Experimental Psychology led by Nick Yeung; and clinicians at OxSTaR led by Helen Higham, focusing on human factors and simulation to support clinical decision-making.
